About Me

Over the last fifteen years I have developed skills in pastry and confections production, as well as the extreme attention to detail required. At a young age, I developed a deep culinary interest, and pursued a culinary career through high school, where I completed the highest-level culinary classes offered. I then attended Central Piedmont Community College, where I earned both the Culinary Arts and Baking and Pastry Arts degrees. During my studies, I honed my skills as a line and prep cook at several restaurants. After graduation, I worked at two country clubs as the pastry chef, I ran a chocoloate shop and also have worked at a boutique hotel. At all of those venues, not only did I plan, delegate, prepare and execute desserts, but also assisted in various stages of savory food preparation for high-volume banquets.

I have an easy-going personality, and the ability to focus under extreme stress to complete projects at the highest standards. I also have experience in menu planning for small to large-scale banquets of up to 600 people. In the past, I have been commended by colleagues and chef instructors on my clear and concise verbal communication abilities, attention to detail, and ability to delegate based on skill levels

Many skills in pastry have developed naturally for me and I have continued to seek out and experiment with new techniques. Of course, this all came with moments of frustration trying to perfect my pastry skills. I recognize that coding may or may not come naturally, but regardless, I must continue to use what I have learned in my past career in regards to planning and breaking projects into smaller tasks in order to reach the desired end goal, and also recognize there will also be moments of frustration, and continue seeking the latest techniques.
